Union Medico Autoinjector Adapter

One of the things I have to do regularly to maintain my health is take an injeciton intramuscularly once a week. While many are okay just doing this free hand, and others go in to their doctor’s offices to get it done, I picked up a Union Medico autoinjector. It’s actually pretty cool, it holds they filled-syringe and drops it in to the muscle at the same pressure every time. It wasn’t super expensive, but has this limitation that it only takes one style of BD Luer-Lok syringe.

Sadly, my pharmacy is out of those syringes so I picked up a bunch in bulk off of Amazon. Unfortuantely, the Amazon product is too small for my auto-injector. Probably should have looked for one with a picture of the syringe on it but even then they don’t have the correct style I need.

To solve this problem, I turned to OpenSCAD, calipers, and my AnkerMake M5 with some PLA filament in the drybox. Let’s get this thing designed! The design was really easy to make, coming in at 10 lines of OpenSCAD and is going to save me a world of hurt not being able to actually take my medicine.

translate([0,0,shank_length]) difference(){
    cylinder(head_length, head_r, head_r);
    #cylinder(head_length, hull_r, hull_r);
        #cube([8,8,head_length]);
}
difference(){
cylinder(shank_length,shank_r, shank_r);
    #cylinder(shank_length, hull_r, hull_r);
    #cube([8,8,shank_length]);
}

Once designed, let’s print it! After printing, you can see the new adapter part next to the syringe.

It fits in to the autoinjector, success!

And makes a NICE snapping sound when clicking in to place, as well as comes off easily after use.

Conclusion

This was a really simple solution to a problem I had in my life
